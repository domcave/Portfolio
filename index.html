Integrating Writing and Design Portfolio

Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

This project was a website that I made to share some facts about myself and my career goals, as well as emphasizing what I thought was good design.

Logo A/B/C

This project were three logos I created with the help of templates from Canva and Adobe. This led directly into our third project.

UX Testing

This project was a lesson in UX testing. I took the three logos I created and interviewed three people about them to collect information. Then, using that information, I created a finalized logo.

Cartoon Portrait

This project introduced me to the tools and features in Adobe Illustrator. I took a picture of myself and turned it into a cartoon.

Good Design

This project had me find an example of good design out in the world and point out the specific peices of it that made it that way. It also challenged me to design the page in an intersting way.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

All of these projects proved to me just how powerful technology is these days in creating. I used both Adobe InDesign and Illustrator, barely scratching the surface of the tools in each and can still realize how game-changing they are. I also gained some low-level knowledge of HTML and CSS. These are programming that format and provide the information within the browser. Every step of the design process involves technology as we move to an increasingly digitalized world.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Proximity, alignment, repetition, and contrast are the four main design principles. We spent a lot of time,both inside and outside of class, talking and analyzing these principles to understand them. And in the Logo project and Good Design project I feel like I was able to get a better grasp on them. While in the Logo project we were just using templates, I could get a good idea of these principles within logos just by scrolling through the options. Then, in the Good Design project, I felt like I finally got to put my skills to the test as I designed my first one-pager keeping those principles in mind.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

Talking about Adobe in class as well as the free alternatives to it really emphasized the ethical concerns of design for me. While Adobe products are extremely refined, they are pay-to-use and the company is extremely predatory. Meanwhile, there is a surplus of free tools out there from the open source community that either match or come extremely close to the Adobe Creative Suite. With how powerful and necessary Adobe can feel while making a project, I have learned that these other options provide the same opportunities for people that may not be able to afford their steep prices. I also became aware of the importance of accesibility options in our webpage, like adding a description of the picture for people who may not be able to see it.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

So far these projects have been fun to bring back to class every day to discuss and share them with my classmates and my professor. I find it especially nice because it allows to me to get great ideas for revision or for future projects by noting techniques, color schemes, or anything else that I liked from my colleagues' projects. I also would consider myself a more social person, so simply having the opportunity to interact with others within the class is nice.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

Continually editing and revising your projects is an integral part of graphic design as you gain more information on your audience, keep up with the new look of the times, or your perspective simply changes. Because of this I am constantly eiditing and revising my projects behind the scenes, trying to simplify yet perfect my design before turning it in. This is more apparent through my Logo designs than anywhere else, as you can clearly see the progression of my design, as well as the information that directly led to its revision.

6. Learn to present your work

Throughout the this class I have not only become more comfortable with presenting my work in front of others, but also doing it in a variety of ways. I have learned how to project my voice and structure my presentations for both large and small groups. I have also learned how to make my presentations protable through the UX Testing project, in which I had to make my findings and revision process easily understandable for somebody that only had access to my slideshow and had not attended my presentation.
